Movie Magic Words

Motivation

Profanity in media is nothing groundbreaking.

In fact, profanity has become such a commonplace in media that Netflix even has a new comedy series History of Swear Words dedicated to explaining and uncovering the origin and impacts of the common foulmouthery. But what of these "magical words" and their place on the big screen? Has the increased frequency of spoken vulgarities become an indicator of a more popular, more trendy, more succesful film? We seek to solve this mystery!

Origins

The f-word is of Germanic origin, related to Dutch, German, and Swedish words for “to strike” and “to move back and forth.” It first appears, though, only in the 16th century, in a manuscript of the Latin orator Cicero.

Origins

The b-word is a pejorative slang word for a person, usually a woman. When applied to a woman or girl, it means someone who is belligerent, unreasonable, malicious, controlling, aggressive, or dominant. When applied to a man or boy, b*tch reverses its meaning and is a derogatory term for being subordinate, weak, or cowardly.
The term b*tch is one of the most common curse words in the English language.

Origins

The S-word is generally considered to be vulgar and profane in Modern English. As a noun, it refers to fecal matter, and as a verb it means to defecate; in the plural ("the sh*ts"), it means diarrhoea. As a slang term, it has many meanings, including: nonsense, foolishness, something of little value or quality, trivial and usually boastful or inaccurate talk or a contemptible person.


Methodology

We used data found on IMSDb and IMDB 5000 Movie Dataset from Kaggle to perform our analysis. Beautiful Soup was used to parse and save all available scripts on IMSDb as a CSV file. Individual script files were then parsed through a function that counted the frequency of f*ck, b*tch, sh*t, and total swear words. We removed duplicate records from the movie dataset and split up the genre variable into a series of dummy variables representing each individual genre. The two datasets were then merged to create the combined dataframe that contains movie and script information.
